projects

Cancellation Comments Categorization

I developed and tuned 5 supervised machine learing models using a TF-IDF framework to categorize coverage cancellation comments under 'Other', into consolidated categories dictated by business decisions. The end deliverable is a Random Forest model that was re-run on the remaining comments to analyze whether current dropdowns are successfully allowing for the customers to select their most relevant options. The breakdown analysis assisted Product Specialists with their decision making, where they adjusted 6 currently available dropdowns to better reflect new customer insights. This was an independent project during my Co-op with Berkshire Hathaway Specialty Insurance - AbracaDATA team at Berxi.

Stack: Python, MSSQL Scikit-Learn, Pandas, Jira

Disaster Communications Assistant, HackMIT 2019

Built on a Raspberry Pi module, the web application is hosted through the Raspberry Pi enviroment on Node-RED, a perfect solution having the ability to process logic and send alerts without Internet connectivity during a natural disaster. I worked with IBM Watson Assistant on the Cloud to develop the speech recognition and logic. Information is then sent and displayed on IBM Cloud Foundry which I built. Other team members built the front-end, using Google Maps API to display heatmaps of highly affected areas.

Stack: IBM Cloud, Node-RED Devpost

Bee Colony and CCD

Incorporated Presticide usage and Bee Colony datasets taken from Kaggle to display their correlations in the United States with Matplotlib visualizations. Constructed machine learning models in Python with Scikit-Learn to predict the type of pesticide used per state and evaluated 3 tuned models based on their Grid Search Cross-Validation and R-Squared scores. This was a group project for DS3000.
