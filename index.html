Cancellation Comments Categorization | ML

Developed 5 supervised machine learing classification models using the TF-IDF framework. These include Random Forest, Naive Bayes, Gradient Boosting, SVM, and Logistic Regression. I collaborated with the Marketing team to reach consolidated categories dictated by business decisions and labeled insurance policy cancellation comments. Inferential Statistical Analysis of Health Care Indexes | OLS Regression, OVB, VIF Multicollinearity

Explored the effects of Purchasing Power on Health Care Indexes in 2020. I analyzed statistical data, with with the help of a correlation matrix, Tableau map, residual graphs, and histograms. Ran OLS Regressions with a Heteroskedasticity-robust Standard Errors fit on 5 side-by-side models then evaluated them based on R-Squared, Omitted Variable Bias, Statistical and Practical Significance, and Variance Inflation Factor Multicollinearity.

Stack: Python, Pandas, Numpy, Statsmodels, Tableau

Bee Colony and Pesticide Use | ML

Explored Presticide Use and Bee Colonies in the United States. Datasets were taken from the USDA where I cleaned over 2k records and 17 predictors using Pandas. Displayed correlations through Matplotlib visualizations. Constructed machine learning models with Scikit-Learn to predict bee population declination per state and evaluated 3 tuned regression models, Lasso, Ridge, and kNN, on their Grid Search Cross-Validation fit and R-Squared scores. This was a final project for DS3000.

Stack: Python, Scikit-Learn, Pandas, Matplotlib

Disaster Communications Assistant | HackMIT

Built on a Raspberry Pi module and hosted through Node-RED on IBM Cloud. Aimed to be the perfect solution in the event of a natural disaster having the ability to process logic and send alerts without Internet connectivity. I worked with IBM Watson Assistant to develop the speech recognition and logic. Information is then sent and displayed on IBM Cloud Foundry dashboard which I built. Other team members built the front-end, using Google Maps API to display heatmaps of highly affected areas. This was a project built over two days at HackMIT 2019.
